is it better to know a little bit of everything or to know everything about a special subject ? certainly many student have already given this question a fair amount of thought . but maybe there is no need for an either / or decision a both approach to knowledge have their advantage in academic life . to advance academic research , you need specialized knowledge in your specific research area . before you can for instance tackle new approach to cancer treatment , you need to know all there is available on your specific subject a a starting point of tought and to form a new and sound hypothesis . many process are highly detailed and not easy to grasp quickly and therefore need an intense effort on the researcher ' side . you do also need broad knowledge of many other academic subject in order to function well in the everyday academic life . in order to communicate with other researcher , you need to know at least the concept of their research subject . broad knowledge also hepls when it come to making your research result understandable to an audience who is not familiar with your specific topic . and lastly , broader knowledge help you seeing the overall picture and pattern that you would miss when you only concentrated on your specific subject . a balance of in-depth knowledge and broad overview is certainly a good basis for meaningful and sustainable research . broad knowledge help you to discovering the general pattern and also communicating with your peer . specialiced knowledge help you to penetrate the problem more profoundly .
